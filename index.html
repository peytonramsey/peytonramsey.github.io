About

I'm a business analytics student specializing in data science and AI engineering, finishing an MS in Business Analytics at UNH (GPA 3.83, May 2026). Most of my work bridges the technical side of building models and the business context they're meant to serve.

I care about the whole problem — building something that fits the need of the actual user, not just something that passes a benchmark. That means understanding what each person actually needs before deciding what to build.

On AI

Working with generative models through my research role has sharpened one view: AI is most effective when treated as a skill, not a shortcut. It can genuinely expand your thinking and accelerate work, but it can just as easily anchor you to mediocre results if you're not skeptical. The practitioners who get the most from it understand its capabilities and its failure modes equally well.

Outside of school, I ran Division I Cross Country and Track at UNH for four years. I'm an avid football fan, enjoy road cycling and lifting, and am currently reading Bad Blood by John Carreyrou.
